A little more about me!

I came to the US in Fall 2022 to pursue a MS in Bioinformatics at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. I had an amazing experience working alongside my mentor and supervisor Dr. Gustavo Caetano-Anoll√©s. I also want to acknowledge my MS thesis committee: Dr. Sandra Rodriguez-Zas and Dr. Maria Bonita Villamil. They have been great mentors and had great input for MS thesis defense.
My research focused on exploring the structural repertoire of major SARS-CoV-2 structural proteins using AlphaFold to model haplotypes that had significant prevalence rates in the population during the COVID-19 pandemic. I am interested in evolutionary bioinformatics and addressing questions related to how proteins evolve and why.
After graduating in Spring 2024 I chose to continue my grad school journey at the University of Maryland, College Park. Unfortunately due to the federal and state budget cuts of 2025 my department lost funding to continue supporting me after my second semester. I am happy to restart my PhD back at the University of Illinois at the GCA Bioinformatics Lab, where I will continue to explore evolutionary bioinformatics.
