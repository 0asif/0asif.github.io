Teaching Experience
I have TA'd a variety of courses since Spring 2023 at the University of Illinois (UIUC) and the University of Maryland (UMD).
Most courses have had a focus on general biology and writing skills.
List of courses taught over the years:
  • HORT 105: Vegetable Gardening Lab (UIUC)
  • PLPA 200: Plants, Pathogens, & People (UIUC)
  • CPSC 116: The Global Food Production Web (UIUC)
  • BSCI 171: Principles of Molecular & Cellular Biology Lab (UMD)
I have been selected to the University of Illinois's List of Teachers Ranked as Outstanding by Their Students for HORT 105 and CPSC 116.


I aim to create an energetic class environment, keeping students engaged and curious. I want my students to not be afraid of making mistakes and to own up to them so that they can continue improving on their journey to becoming young scientists.

Community Resources

There are a wide variety of cultural centers on campus that you can find here: https://newstudent.illinois.edu/connected/cultural-centers. Most of these have weekly events either 12-1 with a lunch provided and numerous other programs that I encourage you all to attend especially if you are a new or international student. Outside of these resources linked below I also want to encourage you to go to: https://one.illinois.edu/club_signup and look for student clubs you find interesting and go to their events. There are many hobby based groups and some identity based groups so there are a lot of options.

Here is a list of some campus units (please let me know if I am missing one) that cater to specific groups of identities as it always helps to find community with people that have similar experiences and these also have opportunities for you to find mentors or programs to help you succeed in academics or otherwise.

  • Asian American Cultural Center (AACC) (https://aacc.illinois.edu/)
  • Bruce D. Nesbitt African American Cultural Center (BNACC) (https://bnaacc.illinois.edu/)
  • La Casa Cultural Latina (https://lacasa.illinois.edu/)
  • Native American House (https://nah.illinois.edu/)
  • Salaam Middle East & North Africa Cultural Center (Or just Salaam Center for short) (https://salaam.illinois.edu/)
  • Womens Resource Center (WRC) (https://wrc.illinois.edu/)
  • Gender and Sexuality Resource Center (GSRC) (https://gsrc.illinois.edu/)

I'll list a few of the weekly lunch (12noon to 1pm) events below (there are many more events):

  • Monday: Dish it up by WRC (every 2nd and 4th Monday) (https://wrc.illinois.edu/programs/dish-it-up) &
    Quench by GSRC (every 1st, 3rd, and 5th Monday) (https://gsrc.illinois.edu/programs/quench)
  • Tuesday: Food for thought by AACC (https://aacc.illinois.edu/programs/food-thought)
  • Wednesday: Food for the soul by BNACC (https://bnaacc.illinois.edu/programs/food-soul)
  • Thursday: Lunch on Us by La Casa (https://lacasa.illinois.edu/programs/lunch-on-us)
  • Friday: Friday forum; Conversation Cafe at the Y (https://www.universityymca.org/what-we-offer/programs/friday-forum-conversation-cafe) (This one is not affiliated with a center but I see many students going to this one)

Health Resources

This is your reminder that you can get allergy and cold medications for FREE (needs iCard) from the mckinley vending machines at the union or one of the libraries or go straight to mckinley if that is closer. At this link you can find all the medications you can get and the locations for the self care vending machines: https://mckinley.illinois.edu/health-wellness/self-care-stations. PS: Stay home if you are sick, let us know by email and we will accomodate you.
